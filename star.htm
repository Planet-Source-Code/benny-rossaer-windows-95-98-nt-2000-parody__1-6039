Everybody in the world has probably seen at least one Star Trek episode. So, what would happen if the USS Enterprise would run under MicroShit WinSux?

After a complete overhaul the USS Enterprise left the refit dock, fitted with a new computer system – Microsoft StarShip 98. Once the ship cleared Earth’s gravity, Picard told Wesley Crusher to lay in a course for the Andolean system. When he seemed unable to do so, Picard asked what the problem was.

‘Sorry Sir,’ said Crusher. ‘It’s this new navigation program, InterPlanet Explorer, I’m not used to it.’

‘InterPlanet Explorer?’ asked Picard. ‘What happened to Planetscape Navigator?’

‘When we installed StarShip 98, InterPlanet Explorer was bundled with it. We were not given the choice to run Planetscape Navigator, Sir,’ explained Data. Picard raised an eyebrow but Wesley managed to enter the course, and Picard gave the order to move to maximum warp. Geordie replied ‘Yes Sir, just a moment.’ ‘Just a moment?’ Picard queried sharply. ‘Sorry Sir,’ said Geordie. ‘When we had the refit they also installed a new antimatter containment field and plasma injectors, and StarShip doesn’t seem to recognise the new hardware.’

‘I thought all Starfleet technology was Plug and Play now,’ mused Picard.

‘In theory Sir,’ said Geordie. ‘But I’m having to find the device drivers manually. Ah, here we go – ANTIMATR.DRV and PLSMINJR.DRV… we are at warp 8, Sir.’

‘Thank goodness for that,’ said Picard. But his relief did not last for long, for as he requested an Earl Grey Tea from the replicator one of the replicator’s circuits burnt out. The computer blurted ‘General system fault’ and the ship was plunged into darkness. ‘What happened?’ Picard shouted. ‘The replicator error crashed the system,’ said Data.

‘The whole system!? An tiny error in a replicator can bring down life support, propulsion, communication and all?’ said Picard incredulously.

‘Yes,’ replied Data. ‘You’ll have to reboot.’

Picard gave the command to reboot the system, and the system enquired whether he wanted to reboot the ship, shut down the ship and log on as a different captain, or simply shut down the computer. No sooner had the computer come back on-line when Worf spoke the chilling words ‘I am detecting a Borg cube on our sensors.’

‘Shields up!’ ordered Picard.

‘Where do I do that?’ asked Worf, bewildered by the unfamiliar console.

‘Try ‘Control Panel’, then ‘Shield Options’,’ said Data helpfully.

‘Hail them!’ demanded Picard.

‘Sorry Sir,’ said Geordie. ‘Our internal communications run on a different protocol to sub space communications, and it seems StarShip 98 performs unpredictably when more than one Comms protocol is installed at once.’

‘Damn!’ yelled Picard, frustrated. ‘Arm torpedoes!’

The computer voice informed him that using torpedoes could result in damage or injury if they were not correctly configured, and said that it was ‘recommended’ that only ‘advanced’ users used these features. Delayed, Picard did not have time to give the order to fire before the Enterprise was rocked by a massive impact. ‘The Borg have opened fire,’ announced Worf superfluously. ‘Damage report!’ demanded Picard.

‘The error occured in Vx[15500:$18890], Sir. It may be possible to recover, Sir. Press any key to return to the system and wait, Sir,’ recited Worf.

‘What?’ cried Picard. ‘Never mind that Vx thing, what decks??’

‘Unknown,’ replied Worf miserably.

‘Fire phasers!’ said Picard desparately.

‘The phasers are not installed as standard, Sir,’ Data reported. ‘They only come as part of the StarShip Plus! pack.’

Before Picard could respond to this latest inconvenience, Worf barked urgently ‘Shield strength is extremely low – the Borg are preparing to board!’

‘This ship is full of Starfleet’s very latest technology! We’ll have to self-destruct; we can’t let these new computer systems fall into Borg hands!’ exclaimed Commander Riker.

‘Why not? It would keep the Borg off our backs,’ observed Picard bitterly, but gave the command to self destruct anyway. The crew scrambled to the escape craft, running down corridors lined with displays reading ‘Please wait while your starship shuts down…’

Picard regarded the screen bearing the words ‘It is now safe to self-destruct your StarShip’ with misery and sadness deep in his heart, before abandoning the Enterprise to its ignoble end.
